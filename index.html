International Technical Working Group on
IoT-Enabled Smart City Framework

Two barriers currently exist to effective and powerful smart city solutions. First, many current smart city ICT deployments are based on custom systems that are not interoperable, portable across cities, extensible, or cost-effective. Second, a number of architectural design efforts are currently underway (e.g. ISO/IEC JTC1, IEC, IEEE, ITU and consortia) but have not yet converged, creating uncertainty among stakeholders. To reduce these barriers, NIST and its partners are convening an international public working group to compare and distill from these architectural efforts and city stakeholders a consensus framework of common architectural features to enable smart city solutions that meet the needs of modern communities.

Welcome to Autumn 2017

This past summer, through two working sessions, we completed refining our methodologies for the working groups. They are currently using these methodologies to complete the data sets that are the object of our efforts. This fall, we must work on our parts to complete the data sets so we can finish the draft of the IES-City Framework. Once we complete this, we will introduce the results in a workshop in the US sometime towards the end of the year. I am currently looking for dates along with my colleague Sokwoo Rhee to connect the two events to the next kickoff of the Global City Team’s Challenge (see https://pages.nist.gov/GCTC/). GCTC just completed its annual expo event in Washington DC and it was a huge success. Of course, track our work at https://pages.nist.gov/smartcitiesarchitecture/.
