International Technical Working Group on
IoT-Enabled Smart City Framework

Two barriers currently exist to effective and powerful smart city solutions. First, many current smart city ICT deployments are based on custom systems that are not interoperable, portable across cities, extensible, or cost-effective. Second, a number of architectural design efforts are currently underway (e.g. ISO/IEC JTC1, IEC, IEEE, ITU and consortia) but have not yet converged, creating uncertainty among stakeholders. To reduce these barriers, NIST and its partners are convening an international public working group to compare and distill from these architectural efforts and city stakeholders a consensus framework of common architectural features to enable smart city solutions that meet the needs of modern communities.

Developing a consensus Framework for Smart City Architectures

According to our plans, it is now fall 2016 and the working groups have embarked on the development of a draft framework.We have created a folder on Google Drive for the editing of the framework.

Here you will find four (4) documents that can be edited on line:

  • IES-CityFrameworkdraft_main.doc that will contain the main sections of the framework. It has some initial content and our collaborators will be adding to it.
  • ApplicationFrameworkReportDraft.doc which contains the document for section 4 of the framework.
  • ConsensusPPIReportDraft.doc which contains the document for section 5 of the framework.
  • DeployedPPIReportDraft.doc which contains the document for section 6 of the framework.

These components of the framework will be built up in the next several weeks to produce a framework draft that will accomplish the following goals:
  • It will establish the look and feel of the final result
  • It will document the methodology devised by each working group to produce their results
  • It will include the results to date from each working group
  • It will provide the basis to attract additional collaborators to produce additional detailed results to broaden out the scope of the analyses
