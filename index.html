International Technical Working Group on
IoT-Enabled Smart City Framework

Two barriers currently exist to effective and powerful smart city solutions. First, many current smart city ICT deployments are based on custom systems that are not interoperable, portable across cities, extensible, or cost-effective. Second, a number of architectural design efforts are currently underway (e.g. ISO/IEC JTC1, IEC, IEEE, ITU and consortia) but have not yet converged, creating uncertainty among stakeholders. To reduce these barriers, NIST and its partners are convening an international public working group to compare and distill from these architectural efforts and city stakeholders a consensus framework of common architectural features to enable smart city solutions that meet the needs of modern communities.

Welcome to Summer 2017

There is a lot going on in the IoT and Smart City space today. We have received acknowledgement for our efforts in several forums and most recently in a draft paper soon to be released by the United States General Services Administration (GSA) on smart city activities in the US and around the world. I personally just came back from speaking at the SynchroniCity and Open & Agile Smart Cities workshop at IoT Week in Geneva on June 6th. At this conference we were able to see that our concepts of Pivotal Points of Interoperability and comparative analyses have intrigued and motivated stakeholders. We will definitely have an eager audience for the tools and other work product from our efforts this past year. I have the goal of producing our first release framework by the end of summer. Our initial plan is to publish this document as a NIST collaborative report available at no charge to anyone at a persistent URL. Other distribution means can be considered as well.

On the other hand, our work is performed by volunteers and the subject matter is challenging. And so our progress has been relatively slower than we hoped. To accelerate our work to the finish line, we are having these two working sessions in Bologna on June 29,30 and in Washington on July 18,19. We hope to attract a serious few volunteers to work with us on the substance of our work product nearing completion.
